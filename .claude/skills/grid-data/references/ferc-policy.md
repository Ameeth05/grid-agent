# FERC Policy Reference

Detailed documentation on FERC Order 2023 and PJM interconnection policies.

## FERC Order 2023 Overview

**Official Name**: Improvements to Generator Interconnection Procedures and Agreements
**Effective Date**: April 2024 (phased implementation)
**Purpose**: Reform interconnection procedures to address multi-year backlogs

### Key Reforms

| Reform | Old Process | New Process |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Study Method | Serial (first-come) | Cluster (first-ready) |
| Financial Commitment | Minimal upfront | Increasing deposits |
| Site Control | 50% at application | 100% at application |
| Timeline | Open-ended | Fixed study windows |
| Withdrawal | Easy at any point | Structured decision points |

## Study Phases

### Phase 1: System Impact Study (SIS)
- **Duration**: ~150 calendar days
- **Purpose**: Initial screening for network constraints
- **Output**: Preliminary cost estimates, identified upgrades
- **Decision Point 1**: Proceed, modify capacity, or withdraw
- **Deposit**: Returned if withdrawn at DP1

### Phase 2: Detailed System Impact Study
- **Duration**: ~150 calendar days
- **Purpose**: Refined analysis with committed projects
- **Output**: Binding cost allocation, firm network upgrades
- **Decision Point 2**: Final commitment or withdrawal
- **Deposit**: Partial forfeit if withdrawn at DP2

### Phase 3: Facilities Study
- **Duration**: ~90 calendar days
- **Purpose**: Final engineering and construction design
- **Output**: Construction-ready drawings, final costs
- **Outcome**: Interconnection agreement (GIA/LGIA)

## Financial Requirements

### Deposit Schedule

| Milestone | Amount | When Due |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Application Deposit | $10,000/MW (min $50,000) | With application |
| Phase 1 Readiness | Additional milestone deposit | Start of Phase 1 |
| Phase 2 Readiness | Additional milestone deposit | Start of Phase 2 |
| Construction Security | Based on allocated upgrade costs | Before construction |

### Withdrawal Refund Schedule

| Decision Point | Refund | Forfeit |
|----------------|--------|---------|
| Before DP1 | 100% of deposits | 0% |
| At DP1 | 50% of deposits | 50% |
| At DP2 | 25% of deposits | 75% |
| After DP2 | 0% of deposits | 100% |

## Site Control Requirements

### Acceptable Documentation
- Executed land purchase agreement
- Executed lease agreement (minimum 30-year term)
- Executed option agreement with right to acquire

### Key Rules
- 100% site control required at application (Order 2023)
- Continuous maintenance throughout study process
- Annual certification required
- Loss of site control = automatic withdrawal

## Common Questions

**Q: Can a project reduce capacity during studies?**
A: Yes, at decision points. Increases generally not allowed.

**Q: What if costs are too high to be economic?**
A: Withdraw at decision point, recover portion of deposits per schedule.

**Q: How are costs allocated among cluster projects?**
A: Proportional to impact - projects causing constraint bear costs.

**Q: What happens if developer misses a milestone?**
A: Risk of PJM-initiated withdrawal (PIW).

**Q: Can a project transfer to a new developer?**
A: Yes, with PJM approval. Successor assumes all obligations.

## Timeline Penalties

### For Transmission Providers (PJM)
- Must complete studies within specified timeframes
- Financial penalties for delays
- Customer entitled to damages for missed deadlines

### For Interconnection Customers
- Must meet milestone deadlines or risk withdrawal
- Site control must be maintained continuously
- Deposits non-refundable after certain points

## Analysis Considerations

When analyzing projects for FERC compliance:

1. **Timeline Risk**: Compare actual dates to Order 2023 requirements
2. **Milestone Status**: Check each study phase completion
3. **Cost Trajectory**: Monitor for significant cost increases
4. **Withdrawal Patterns**: Look for signs of financial distress
5. **Modification Requests**: Flag capacity or timeline changes
